<p><p>The wheat malt market can be divided into two primary types: white malt and red malt. White malt is produced from pale wheat varieties and is typically lighter in color, offering a mild flavor profile. It is commonly used in brewing light beers and crafting delicate baked goods. In contrast, red malt is derived from darker wheat varieties, resulting in a richer color and a more robust flavor. This type is favored for producing darker beers and specialty baked items, enhancing the overall quality and diversity of wheat malt products.</p></p>
