<p><p>The High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) power supply market can be categorized into two main types: Positive Polarity HVDC and Negative Polarity HVDC. Positive Polarity HVDC systems transmit power with a higher voltage on the positive terminal, facilitating long-distance energy transfer with lower losses. In contrast, Negative Polarity HVDC systems operate with the negative terminal at a higher voltage, which can enhance system stability and reduce electromagnetic interference. Both types are essential for efficient transmission in modern power grids.</p></p>
