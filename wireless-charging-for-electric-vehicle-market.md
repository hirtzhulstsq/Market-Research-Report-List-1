<p><ul><li>Electromagnetic Induction</li><li>Magnetic Resonance</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Wireless charging for electric vehicles primarily consists of two market types: electromagnetic induction and magnetic resonance. Electromagnetic induction uses magnetic fields generated by coils to transfer energy between a charging pad and the vehicle, requiring precise alignment. In contrast, magnetic resonance allows for greater spatial freedom, using resonant inductive coupling to transfer energy more efficiently over longer distances. Both technologies aim to enhance convenience and user experience in EV charging, supporting the growth of sustainable transportation solutions.</p></p>
