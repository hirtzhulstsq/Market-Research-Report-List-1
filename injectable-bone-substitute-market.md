<p><p>The Injectable Bone Substitute market comprises two primary types: natural and synthetic materials. Natural substitutes are derived from biological sources, such as autografts, allografts, or xenografts, providing biocompatibility and enhanced healing properties. Synthetic substitutes, on the other hand, are man-made materials, including calcium phosphate and bioactive glass, designed to mimic the structural integrity of bone and promote regeneration. Both types are utilized in various orthopedic and dental procedures, offering options for tailored patient treatment and recovery.</p></p>
